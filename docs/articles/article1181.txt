Protein powders are commonly used to increase muscle mass or support weight loss.
They’re also helpful for women who want a quick and convenient way to add more protein to their diet.
However, with so many brands, types, and flavors on the market, it can be overwhelming trying to decide which powder to choose.
The products included in this article were selected based on the following criteria:
Third-party testing.  We looked for products that are third-party tested to ensure safety and quality.
Quality ingredients.  In addition to the type and quality of ingredients used, we included powers with a limited use of fillers, additives, and artificial ingredients.
Nutritional value.  We considered the nutritional value of each product, specifically looking for products that are high in protein and low in added sugar.
Brand quality.  We vetted each brand and product to ensure they meet our business and medical standards.
Also, keep in mind that while these products are either marketed toward women or contain ingredients that are particularly beneficial for women, the protein powders listed below can be enjoyed by most healthy adults.

How to choose the best protein powder for you
There are several factors to consider when  shopping for a protein powder .
First, it’s important to decide the type of protein that you prefer.
While whey protein is popular among those looking to gain muscle, those following a vegan or dairy-free diet should choose a  plant-based alternative  that’s sourced from ingredients like peas, brown rice, or chia seeds.
You should also look for a supplement that offers a variety of flavors and is suitable for however you plan to use it, whether that’s in baked goods, protein shakes, or smoothies.
You may also want to opt for a protein powder that contains nutrients and ingredients that offer  specific benefits for women , including probiotics, B vitamins, and iron.
Be sure to also review the nutrition label carefully and opt for a product that meets your nutritional needs, especially if you’re looking to increase or decrease your intake of sugar, fat, fiber, carbs, or specific vitamins or minerals.
Finally, products should ideally be tested by a third-party company, such as NSF International or Informed Choice, to ensure that the powder contains the type and amount of ingredients it claims to on the label.

The bottom line
Whether you’re looking to build muscle or need help meeting your protein needs, several great protein powder options are available for women — and they’re suitable for most healthy adults, too.
To help narrow down your search, be sure to consider the type, flavor, and nutritional value of each product. It’s also best to opt for supplements that are third-party tested to ensure safety and quality.
Of course, keep in mind that protein powders are not a substitute for a healthy, balanced diet. In fact, most women can meet their protein needs by eating  a variety of protein-rich foods .
