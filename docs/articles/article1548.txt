Title: 20 Delicious Low Carb Breakfast Recipes

From bagels to toast to biscuits and gravy, many of the most beloved breakfast foods are loaded with carbs.
If you’re following a low carb or ketogenic diet, it can therefore be incredibly challenging to determine what to eat for your morning meal.
Fortunately, numerous breakfast options are low in carbs and still full of flavor.
Here are 20 low carb breakfast recipes you can try to get your day going on the right foot.

1–8. Egg dishes
3 large eggs
2 tablespoons (30 mL) of heavy cream
1 cup (30 grams) of spinach
1 ounce (28 grams) of  goat cheese
1 tablespoon (14 grams) of butter
salt and pepper to taste
Whisk the eggs, heavy cream, salt, and pepper in a bowl, then set the mixture aside.
Melt the butter in a pan over medium heat and sauté the  spinach . Stir occasionally until the spinach is dark green and wilted. Remove from the pan and set aside.
Pour the egg mixture into the pan and cook it over medium heat.
Once the eggs start to set, flip them over using a spatula.
Add spinach and goat cheese to half of the omelet. Once the omelet has set, fold it in half and serve.
6 large eggs
1/3 cup (80 mL) of milk
salt and pepper to taste
4 cloves of  garlic , minced
2 cups (60 grams) of kale, chopped
2 cups (300 grams) of bell peppers, chopped
1 ounce (28 grams) of crumbled feta
1 tablespoon (15 mL) of olive oil
Preheat the oven to 400°F (205°C).
Whisk the eggs, milk, minced garlic, salt, and pepper, then set the mixture aside.
Add the olive oil to a 10-inch (25-cm), oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Sauté the chopped kale and  bell peppers , stirring occasionally until softened, or about 5–7 minutes.
Pour in the egg mixture and tilt the skillet to distribute it evenly. Sprinkle the feta over the top.
Bake 15–20 minutes in the skillet until the eggs are set, then season, slice, and serve.
1 avocado
2 large eggs
salt and pepper to taste
optional toppings, such as bacon bits, cheese, chives,  cilantro , or tomatoes
Preheat the oven to 400°F (205°C).
Slice the  avocado  in half, remove the pit, and use a spoon to scoop out some flesh from the middle and make the hole bigger.
Crack one egg into each hole.
Season with salt, pepper, and add your choice of toppings.
Bake for 10-15 minutes and serve.
6 large eggs
1 onion, diced
1 red bell pepper, diced
3 cloves of garlic, minced
a 28-ounce (794-gram) can of crushed tomatoes
salt and pepper to taste
1 tablespoon (7 grams) of paprika
1/2 teaspoon (1 gram) of red chili powder
1 teaspoon (2 grams) of  cumin
2 ounces (57 grams) of crumbled feta
1 bunch of cilantro, chopped
Add oil to a pan over medium heat. Sauté diced onions and bell peppers for 3–5 minutes, or until the onions turn translucent.
Pour the canned tomatoes into the pan with juice. Stir in the minced garlic, salt, pepper, and spices and bring the mixture to a simmer.
Using a spoon, make 6 small holes in the mixture and crack 1 egg into each.
Cover the pan and cook for 5–10 minutes, or until the eggs have reached your desired level of doneness.
Top with  feta  and cilantro and serve.
6 large eggs
1 cup (240 mL) of milk
1 cup (90 grams) of  broccoli , chopped
4 slices of bacon, chopped
3/4 cup (85 grams) of shredded cheddar cheese
2 tablespoons (30 mL) of olive oil
salt and pepper to taste
Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
Cook the  bacon  in a pan over medium heat for 5–10 minutes, or until crispy. Set it aside on a towel-lined plate.
Add the olive oil to the pan and sauté the chopped broccoli for 3–4 minutes. Distribute the broccoli and bacon evenly at the bottom of a greased 9-inch (23-cm) pie dish.
In a small bowl, whisk the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper. Pour the egg mixture over the pie dish and top it with cheese.
Bake for 30–35 minutes until the eggs have set. Cool before cutting and serving.
2  zucchinis , spiralized
2 large eggs
1 tablespoon (30 mL) of olive oil
salt and pepper to taste
optional toppings, such as feta cheese, sliced avocado, or red pepper flakes
Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
Add the olive oil to a pan and sauté the zucchini over medium heat for 2–3 minutes, or until tender.
Arrange each spiralized zucchini in a skillet to create a nest. Crack an egg in the center, sprinkle with salt and  pepper , and bake for 5 minutes.
Add desired toppings and serve.
12 large eggs
1/3 cup (80 mL) of milk
1/4 cup (29 grams) of red onion, diced
1 cup (70 grams) of  mushrooms , diced
1 cup (150 grams) of bell peppers, diced
1/2 cup (90 grams) of tomatoes, diced
1 cup (110 grams) of shredded cheese
2 tablespoons (30 mL) of olive oil
salt and pepper to taste
Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
Add the olive oil to a pan and sauté the vegetables over medium heat for 5 minutes, or until softened.
Scoop the  vegetables  and cheese evenly into a greased, 12-cup muffin tin.
In a small bowl, mix the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper. Distribute the mixture equally into each cup of the muffin tin.
Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until set, and cool before serving.
2 large eggs
2 tablespoons (30 mL) of  heavy cream
1 tablespoon (14 grams) of butter
salt and pepper to taste
1 turkey sausage patty
In a small bowl, whisk the eggs, heavy cream, salt, and pepper.
Add butter to a pan over medium-low heat and pour in the egg mixture.
Once the edges of the eggs start to set, use a spatula to push the eggs from side to side and break up the curds. Continue until the eggs are mostly cooked and remove from heat.
Cook the  turkey sausage patty  in a pan over medium heat and serve alongside scrambled eggs.

9–12. Sweets and baked goods
2 large eggs
2 ounces (57 grams) of  cream cheese
1–2 teaspoons (2–4 grams) of stevia
1 teaspoon (5 mL) of vanilla extract
2 tablespoons (30 mL) of olive oil
Add the ingredients to a blender or food processor and pulse until smooth.
Heat a pan over medium heat and add the olive oil.
Pour 1/4 of the batter into the pan and cook for 2–3 minutes, until golden.
Flip and cook for 1 minute, or until the second side is golden.
Repeat with the rest of the batter and top with butter, sugar-free syrup, sugar-free jam,  fresh berries , or yogurt.
1/2 cup (75 grams) of almonds
1/2 cup (60 grams) of walnuts
1/4 cup (40 grams) of hemp seeds
1/4 cup (23 grams) of unsweetened coconut flakes
1/4 cup (43 grams) of sugar-free chocolate chips 1/4 cup (60 grams) of almond butter
1 large egg
4 tablespoons (48 grams) of  monk fruit extract
2 teaspoons (10 mL) of vanilla extract
a pinch of salt
Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
Add the almonds and walnuts to a food processor. Pulse a few times until the nuts are chopped into large pieces.
Combine the chopped nuts with hemp seeds, coconut flakes, salt, chocolate chips, and monk fruit in a large bowl and set the mixture aside.
In a separate bowl, mix the egg, vanilla extract, and almond butter. Whisk and pour the mixture into the larger bowl with the nuts and seeds.
Press the mixture firmly into a baking sheet lined with parchment paper until the  granola mixture  is very compact.
Bake 15–20 minutes until fully set, then cool completely before cutting and serving.
2 cups (224 grams) of almond flour
1/4 cup (30 grams) of low carb protein powder
1/2 cup (100 grams) of  granulated sugar substitute , such as monk fruit
1 teaspoon (5 grams) of baking powder
4 tablespoons (56 grams) of butter, melted
1/2 cup (120 mL) of unsweetened nut milk
3 large eggs
1 teaspoon (5 mL) of vanilla extract
2/3 cup (115 grams) of sugar-free chocolate chips
Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
Line a muffin tin with cupcake liners.
Mix the almond flour, protein powder, sugar substitute, and baking powder in a large bowl.
Slowly mix in the melted  butter , nut milk, eggs, and vanilla extract, followed by the chocolate chips.
Spoon the batter evenly into each lined muffin cup. Bake for 20–25 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean from the center of a muffin.
1/2 cup (56 grams) of  almond flour
4 large eggs
1 teaspoon (5 grams) of baking powder
2 tablespoons (24 grams) of monk fruit extract
1/3 cup (82 grams) of Greek yogurt
1 tablespoon (14 grams) of butter, melted
Add the ingredients to a blender or food processor and blend until smooth.
Heat a waffle maker. Add 1/4 of the batter and cook until waffles become golden brown and crispy on the edges.
Repeat with the remainder of the batter and serve topped with berries, sugar-free syrup, or sugar-free  jam .

13–14. Pudding and yogurt
3/4 cup (180 mL) of  coconut milk
1/4 cup (43 grams) of chia seeds
1/2 tablespoon (7 mL) of sugar-free maple syrup
1/2 teaspoon (2.5 mL) of vanilla extract
Add the ingredients to a medium bowl and whisk to combine.
Cover and refrigerate the mixture overnight, or at least 6–8 hours.
Portion and serve as is, or top it with your choice of ingredients, such as fresh fruit, nuts, or  seeds .
1 cup (245 grams) of  Greek yogurt
1/2 cup (83 grams) of your favorite low carb fruit, such as strawberries, blackberries, or raspberries
optional toppings, such as almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, or flaxseeds
Combine the yogurt and fresh fruit. Top with  nuts  and seeds if desired and serve.

15–16. Vegetable dishes
2 cups (60 grams) of spinach
2  hard-boiled  eggs
1 ounce (28 grams) of mozzarella
1 Roma tomato, diced
half of an avocado, sliced
2 tablespoons (30 mL) of olive oil
1 tablespoon (15 mL) of lemon juice
salt and pepper to taste
Add the spinach to a bowl and layer with the eggs, mozzarella, tomatoes, and avocados.
To make the  dressing , whisk the olive oil with lemon juice and a dash of salt and pepper.
Drizzle the dressing over the salad and serve.
2 large bell peppers
4 large  eggs
1/2 cup (55 grams) of shredded cheese
salt and pepper to taste
2 strips of bacon, cooked and crumbled
1 cup (30 grams) of spinach, chopped
Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
Cut each bell pepper in half length-wise and remove the stem and seeds. Arrange them on a greased baking sheet and set aside.
In a small bowl, whisk the eggs, bacon, spinach, salt, pepper, and 1/4 cup (28 grams) of  cheese .
Spoon the egg mixture into each bell pepper evenly. Top with the remaining cheese.
Bake for 35–45 minutes, or until the eggs are set.

17–18. Vegan dishes
16 ounces (454 grams) of extra-firm tofu
2 tablespoons (22 grams) of  nutritional yeast
2 tablespoons (28 mL) of olive oil
half of a red onion, chopped
half of a bell pepper, chopped
4 cloves of garlic, minced
2 cups (60 grams) of spinach, chopped
salt and pepper to taste
Drain the tofu and remove any remaining moisture by patting the block dry, setting it on a plate covered with a paper towel, and placing a heavy object on top to press it for 10–15 minutes.
Use a fork to crumble the tofu into small pieces in a bowl and set aside.
Add the  olive oil  to a pan over medium heat and then sauté the onions and garlic for 2–3 minutes.
Add the bell pepper and spinach. Sauté until the spinach is dark green and wilted.
Add the crumbled tofu to the pan and cook for 3–4 minutes, stirring occasionally to break up large clumps.
Mix in the nutritional yeast and season with salt and pepper before serving.

juice from half of a  lemon
salt and pepper to taste

19–20. Fruit and smoothies
2 cups (60 grams) of spinach
half of an avocado
1 cup (165 grams) of  strawberries
1–1.5 cups (240–350 mL) of unsweetened almond milk
1 scoop of  low carb protein powder  (optional)
Add the ingredients to a blender and blend until smooth.
1 apple, cut into rings
2 tablespoons (30 grams) of peanut butter
optional toppings, such as chopped almonds, pecans, walnuts,  cinnamon , or sugar-free chocolate chips
Spread  peanut butter  evenly over apple rings.
Sprinkle on your choice of toppings and enjoy.

The bottom line
A variety of scrumptious, nutritious low carb breakfast options can spice up your morning.
Try experimenting with some of the recipes above to reduce your carb intake and diversify your diet. You can also get creative in the kitchen and swap in your favorite ingredients to tailor these low carb dishes to your taste.
Try this today:  Non-starchy vegetables, nuts, seeds, eggs, and dairy products all make great additions to any low carb breakfast. Check out  this list  for more healthy, low carb foods to stock up on.
